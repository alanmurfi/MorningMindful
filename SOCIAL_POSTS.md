### r/digitalminimalism

**Title:** Built an app to break my morning phone addiction - and it actually worked

**Body:**
I used to lose 1-2 hours every morning to social media before even getting out of bed. Screen Time limits didn't work - I'd just dismiss them.

So I built Morning Mindful. Instead of blocking apps entirely (which felt restrictive), it redirects you to a simple journal. Write a few sentences about your thoughts, gratitude, or plans for the day, and the block lifts.

The psychology behind it:
- **Friction, not restriction** - You CAN access apps, just not immediately
- **Positive replacement** - Instead of doom-scrolling, you reflect
- **Morning ritual** - Journaling becomes your new first activity
- **No guilt** - Once you journal, scroll all you want

After 2 weeks of using it myself:
- Morning screen time down 70%
- Actually remember what I was grateful for
- Feel more intentional about my day

It's free on Google Play. Would love feedback from this community.

## Posting Strategy

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Week 1-2)
1. **r/androidapps** - Primary discovery, ask for feedback
2. **r/digitalminimalism** - Highly relevant audience
3. Wait for feedback, fix any bugs reported

### Phase 2: Expand (Week 3-4)
1. **r/productivity** - Broader audience
2. **r/journaling** - Niche but engaged
3. **Twitter/X** - Multiple tweets over the week
4. Respond to all comments promptly

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 2+)
1. **Product Hunt** - Once you have ~100+ active users
2. **LinkedIn** - Professional audience
3. **r/getdisciplined** - Relevant community
4. **r/selfimprovement** - Relevant community

### Tips
- Post on weekday mornings (EST) for best visibility
- Always add Play Store link in comments, not post body (spam filters)
- Respond to EVERY comment within 24 hours
- Ask genuine questions to encourage engagement
- Be honest about it being your app
- Don't spam - one post per subreddit
